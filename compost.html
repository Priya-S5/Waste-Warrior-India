🧠 What Is Composting?

Composting is the natural process of recycling organic matter—like food scraps and garden waste—into a nutrient-rich soil conditioner. It reduces landfill waste, lowers methane emissions, and nourishes local gardens and farms.

🌱 What Can Be Composted?

  • ✔️ Vegetable peels, fruit scraps, tea leaves
  • ✔️ Coffee grounds, eggshells, bread
  • ✔️ Garden clippings, dry leaves, sawdust
  • ✔️ Newspaper (shredded), cardboard (uncoated)

⚠️ Avoid: dairy, meat, oily foods, synthetic materials

🧪 Composting Methods

1. Pit Composting: Dig a shallow pit and layer wet and dry waste. Cover with soil and let nature do the work.

2. Bin Composting: Use a closed bin with air holes. Stir weekly and monitor moisture.

3. Vermicomposting: Introduce red worms to accelerate decomposition. Ideal for small spaces.

🌡️ Composting Conditions

✦ Maintain a balance of “greens” (wet waste) and “browns” (dry waste)
✦ Keep it moist—not soggy
✦ Stir weekly to aerate
✦ Ideal temperature: 40–60°C for active composting
